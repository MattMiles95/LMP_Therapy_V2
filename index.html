Lewis Murphy-Parry

Psychotherapist – Individual psychotherapy & supervision

Psychotherapy offers a dedicated space to explore the patterns, difficulties and questions that shape your life. Over time, the therapeutic relationship allows us to look closely at long-standing concerns and the structures that hold them in place.

My approach is integrative. I draw on existential–phenomenological perspectives, which focus on lived experience, freedom and responsibility, alongside ideas from Lacanian psychoanalysis, which attend to family structures, desire and the unconscious. Together, these frameworks help us work with both the immediate felt sense of your situation and the deeper structures underlying it.

Areas of experience

I have worked with a wide range of concerns, which can include:

  • relationship or family difficulties;
  • pressure and stress related to work or career;
  • questions of confidence, identity and self-esteem;
  • issues around sex, sexuality and intimacy;
  • feelings of anxiety, low mood or lack of direction;
  • experiences of loss, change or transition.

My practice is based in Somewhere, London, and I offer sessions either in person or online (via Zoom).

If you would like to discuss starting therapy or have any questions, you are welcome to contact me by email: therapy@lmp.com.

About

I trained as a psychotherapist at a University, and hold a Master's degree in Psychotherapy awarded by a University. I am a fully accredited member of the UK Council for Psychotherapy (UKCP).

Alongside my clinical work, I provide individual and group supervision for psychotherapists and trainees.

Before working as a psychotherapist I spent several years doing other things. These experiences have informed my understanding of the pressures and demands that can arise in different professional and personal contexts.
